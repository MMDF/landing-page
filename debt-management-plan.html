Debt Management Plan (DMP)

A debt management plan, also known as a DMP, is an informal solution designed to reduce your monthly payments towards your debts.

A Debt Management Plan is an agreement, negotiated by you or a third party, to lower your monthly payments to your creditors. It isn’t legally binding, unlike other debt solutions, such as an Individual Voluntary Arrangement (IVA) or a Trust Deed. With a DMP, your monthly payments are proposed based on what you can reasonably afford.

Benefits of an DMP

  • You’ll only pay what you can afford to your creditors. after putting together a monthly household budget.
  • You’ll make one monthly payment and payments to your creditors will be managed for you.
  • Your DMP will be regularly reviewed to make sure you’re paying what you can afford.

Risks of an DMP

  • Some of your creditors may still contact you
  • Most creditors will agree to reduce or stop interest and charges, but they don’t have to
  • If creditors continue adding interest and charges, this could increase the total amount you currently owe
  • Your creditors don’t have to agree to your reduced payments, but your payments send to your creditors anyway.
  • Your creditors may still take further court action against you, such as a County Court judgment (CCJ)
